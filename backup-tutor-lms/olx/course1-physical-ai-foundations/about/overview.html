Physical AI Foundations

From simulator to AI-controlled robot — the complete pipeline.

Physical AI is the convergence of robotics and machine learning. Instead of programming every behavior by hand, robots now learn from human demonstrations, simulation, and feedback. This course teaches you the complete pipeline — from understanding what a robot perceives, to collecting training data, to deploying a policy that controls real hardware.

What you'll learn

  • The difference between classical robotics and Physical AI
  • How simulation environments generate training data at scale
  • How teleoperation produces expert demonstrations
  • How to design and clean a robot dataset
  • How behavior cloning and vision policies work
  • What VLM and VLA models are and how they're used
  • How to close the sim-to-real gap

Who this is for

  • ML engineers moving into robotics
  • Robotics engineers who want to add AI
  • Startup builders and researchers
  • Final-year engineering students

Prerequisites

Basic Python. Curiosity about robots. No robotics background required.

Duration

8 modules · ~6 hours · Self-paced
