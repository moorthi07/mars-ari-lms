Module 5 — Arm & Manipulator Controls

Arm controls let you move a robot's arm and gripper. This is harder than drive controls — but it's where the most valuable training data comes from.

The Arm Joystick

Controls the arm's end-effector (the gripper/hand) in 3D space. Think of it like moving your own hand — you're controlling where the gripper goes, not each individual joint.

6-DOF Sliders

Six sliders, one for each joint of the arm. Each slider moves one joint. This gives you precise control but requires more coordination. Good for fine adjustments.

SVG Arm Control

A visual representation of the arm. Click and drag joints directly on the diagram. Intuitive for beginners.

The Gripper

Open and close the gripper using the gripper control. Most tasks involve: approach → open gripper → move to object → close gripper → lift → move to target → open gripper → release.

Joint Limits

Each joint has a safe range of motion. The platform enforces these limits automatically — you can't move a joint past its safe range. If a slider stops moving, you've hit a limit. Don't force it.

Tips for Good Arm Data

  • Move slowly and deliberately — no sudden jerks
  • Approach objects from a consistent direction every time
  • Close the gripper fully before lifting
  • Don't hover — commit to the movement
